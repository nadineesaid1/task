Hashing vs Encryption

Hashing and encryption are both techniques used to secure data, but they serve different purposes and operate in distinct ways.

What is Encryption?

Encryption is the process of converting data into a coded format that can only be read with a specific key. It is a two-way function, meaning the original data can be retrieved by decrypting the encrypted data.

What is Hashing?

Hashing is the process of converting data into a fixed-size string of characters, typically a hash code. It is a one-way function, meaning the original data cannot be reversed once hashed.

Key Differences

  • Purpose: Encryption protects confidentiality, while hashing ensures integrity.
  • Reversibility: Encryption is reversible with a key, hashing is irreversible.
  • Output: Encryption output varies, hashing always produces fixed-length output.

Use Cases

Encryption is used to secure sensitive information like passwords, communication, and banking data.

Hashing is used for password storage, data verification, and digital signatures.

Password Storage Safety

Passwords should generally be stored as hashes rather than encrypted values. Strong hashing algorithms like bcrypt, scrypt, or Argon2 with salting provide much stronger protection against attacks.

Conclusion

Encryption and hashing are both essential cybersecurity techniques. Encryption protects confidentiality, while hashing ensures integrity and verification. For password storage, strong hashing algorithms with unique salts are the recommended choice.
